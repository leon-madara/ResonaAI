# Kenya Data Protection Act 2019 Compliance Checklist
## Mental Health Platform for East Africa

### Overview

This document outlines the compliance requirements and implementation status for the Mental Health Platform under Kenya's Data Protection Act 2019 (DPA). The platform processes sensitive personal data, including mental health information, requiring strict adherence to data protection principles.

### Legal Framework

**Primary Legislation**: Data Protection Act, No. 24 of 2019  
**Regulatory Authority**: Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)  
**Effective Date**: November 25, 2019  
**Key Regulations**: Data Protection (General) Regulations, 2021

### Compliance Checklist

#### 1. Registration and Notification

- [ ] **Register with ODPC** (Section 18)
  - Submit registration application within 6 months of processing
  - Pay registration fees (KSh 4,000 for data controllers)
  - Provide detailed processing activities description
  - **Status**: Pending - To be completed before launch
  - **Responsible**: Legal Team
  - **Deadline**: 6 months before launch

- [ ] **Appoint Data Protection Officer** (Section 24)
  - Designate qualified DPO
  - Ensure DPO independence and resources
  - Provide DPO contact details to ODPC
  - **Status**: Pending - DPO to be appointed
  - **Responsible**: Management
  - **Deadline**: Before registration

#### 2. Lawful Basis for Processing

- [ ] **Explicit Consent** (Section 30)
  - Obtain clear, specific consent for sensitive data
  - Provide detailed information about processing
  - Allow easy withdrawal of consent
  - **Implementation**: Granular consent management system
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Vital Interests** (Section 30(2)(c))
  - Document crisis intervention procedures
  - Establish clear criteria for vital interest processing
  - Implement safeguards for emergency processing
  - **Implementation**: Crisis detection and intervention system
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

#### 3. Data Subject Rights

- [ ] **Right to Information** (Section 26)
  - Provide clear privacy policy in English and Swahili
  - Explain data processing purposes and legal basis
  - Inform about data subject rights
  - **Implementation**: Multi-language privacy policy
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Legal Team

- [ ] **Right of Access** (Section 26)
  - Provide access to personal data within 21 days
  - Explain data sources and processing logic
  - Provide data in understandable format
  - **Implementation**: User dashboard with data access
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Right to Rectification** (Section 27)
  - Allow data correction within 21 days
  - Notify third parties of corrections
  - Provide confirmation of corrections
  - **Implementation**: Data correction interface
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Right to Erasure** (Section 28)
  - Allow data deletion within 21 days
  - Implement secure deletion procedures
  - Provide deletion confirmation
  - **Implementation**: Account deletion with secure data removal
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Right to Restrict Processing** (Section 29)
  - Allow processing restriction
  - Explain restriction implications
  - Provide easy reactivation
  - **Implementation**: Processing control interface
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Right to Data Portability** (Section 31)
  - Provide data in machine-readable format
  - Allow data transfer to other providers
  - Explain data format and structure
  - **Implementation**: Data export functionality
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

#### 4. Data Protection Principles

- [ ] **Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency** (Section 25)
  - Ensure all processing has legal basis
  - Process data fairly and transparently
  - Provide clear information about processing
  - **Implementation**: Comprehensive privacy framework
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Legal and Development Teams

- [ ] **Purpose Limitation** (Section 25)
  - Collect data for specified purposes only
  - Prevent function creep
  - Document all processing purposes
  - **Implementation**: Purpose-based access controls
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Data Minimization** (Section 25)
  - Collect only necessary data
  - Regularly review data collection
  - Implement data minimization by design
  - **Implementation**: Minimal data collection approach
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Accuracy** (Section 25)
  - Ensure data accuracy and currency
  - Implement data validation
  - Allow easy data correction
  - **Implementation**: Data validation and correction systems
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Storage Limitation** (Section 25)
  - Retain data only as long as necessary
  - Implement automated deletion
  - Document retention periods
  - **Implementation**: Configurable retention periods
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Integrity and Confidentiality** (Section 25)
  - Implement appropriate security measures
  - Protect against unauthorized access
  - Ensure data confidentiality
  - **Implementation**: End-to-end encryption and access controls
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Accountability** (Section 25)
  - Document compliance measures
  - Implement privacy by design
  - Conduct regular audits
  - **Implementation**: Comprehensive documentation and auditing
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: All Teams

#### 5. Security Measures

- [ ] **Technical Safeguards** (Section 41)
  - Implement encryption for data at rest and in transit
  - Use secure authentication methods
  - Implement access controls and logging
  - **Implementation**: AES-256 encryption, JWT authentication
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **Organizational Safeguards** (Section 41)

  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: HR and Legal Teams

#### 6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

- [ ] **Adequate Protection** (Section 48)
  - Ensure adequate protection in destination countries
  - Implement appropriate safeguards
  - Document transfer mechanisms
  - **Implementation**: Data anonymization and encryption for transfers
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

- [ ] **User Consent** (Section 48)
  - Obtain explicit consent for transfers
  - Provide information about transfer risks
  - Allow withdrawal of transfer consent
  - **Implementation**: Transfer consent in privacy policy
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Legal Team

#### 7. Data Protection Impact Assessment

- [ ] **Conduct DPIA** (Section 31)
  - Assess privacy risks of processing
  - Document mitigation measures
  - Review and update regularly
  - **Implementation**: Comprehensive DPIA document
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Legal and Development Teams

#### 8. Breach Notification

- [ ] **72-Hour Notification** (Section 43)
  - Notify ODPC within 72 hours of breach
  - Provide detailed breach information
  - Cooperate with ODPC investigation
  - **Implementation**: Incident response procedures
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Legal and Development Teams

- [ ] **User Notification** (Section 43)
  - Notify users of high-risk breaches
  - Provide clear information about breach
  - Offer mitigation advice
  - **Implementation**: User notification system
  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Development Team

#### 9. Records of Processing Activities

- [ ] **Maintain Records** (Section 32)

  - **Status**: In Development
  - **Responsible**: Legal Team

#### 10. Children's Data Protection

### Risk Assessment

#### High-Risk Areas
1. **Sensitive Data Processing**: Mental health data requires extra protection
2. **Cross-Border Transfers**: AI services may involve international transfers
3. **Crisis Intervention**: Emergency processing may bypass normal consent
4. **Children's Data**: Special protection required for minors

#### Mitigation Strategies
1. **Enhanced Encryption**: Use strongest available encryption
2. **Data Anonymization**: Remove PII before international transfers
3. **Clear Procedures**: Document all emergency processing
4. **Age Verification**: Implement robust age verification system
